## Idea: Core Positioning Statement — Hako Nomad

- Idea: "For everyday listeners who care how their space looks, the Hako Nomad is the portable speaker that earns its place on your shelf — because it sounds as good as it looks, without the price premium or app frustrations of Marshall."
- Product Line: Hako Nomad
- Status: Idea
- Notes: Sourced from Round 1 Reddit intelligence. What to own: the intersection of honest aesthetics and reliable sound at a price that always feels fair — the premium look you actually keep. What to avoid: ruggedness/outdoor toughness (JBL/UE/Tribit territory), out-speccing Sony or Bose on pure audio performance. Pending analyst review before advancing to Testing.
- Date Added: 2026-03-18

## Idea: White Space — Retro Aesthetic + Post-Purchase Reliability

- Idea: Own "the retro-aesthetic portable speaker that is equally reliable post-purchase" — looks like Marshall, works like Bose, supported like JBL.
- Product Line: Hako Nomad
- Status: Idea
- Notes: No competitor currently holds this combination. Marshall owns the aesthetic but is documented as unreliable. JBL is reliable but aesthetically rejected for home use. Bose is reliable and sounds good but visually generic. This is the Hako Nomad's unoccupied white space. Pending analyst review.
- Date Added: 2026-03-18

## Idea: Honest Pricing — "The Price That's Always the Price"

- Idea: Own transparent, always-fair pricing — no sale theater, no markdown from an inflated MSRP.
- Product Line: Hako Nomad
- Status: Idea
- Notes: Marshall's Emberton at $180 full price generates documented buyer friction. At $90 (50% off) it becomes an impulse buy — buyers feel overcharged at full price. A new entrant priced at $99-$129 with no theatrical markdowns can own "fair price, always." No current competitor holds this position in the aesthetic tier. Pending analyst review.
