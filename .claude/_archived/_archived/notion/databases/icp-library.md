## The Room Builder

- Persona Name: The Room Builder
- Product Line: Hako Nomad
- Age Range: 27-38
- Key Traits: Apartment or small home dweller who designs their space deliberately. Owns considered objects — good lamp, mid-century chair, kept-alive plant. Follows r/malelivingspace or interior accounts on Instagram. Not rich, but deliberate about purchases. Music is part of daily life. Does not self-identify as an audiophile. Discovers via YouTube then validates on Reddit. Rejects JBL for home use on aesthetic grounds. Frustrated by Marshall's locked EQ, app failures, and post-warranty service. Wants "warm punchy sound" and "late-night vibe" — not audiophile accuracy. Price comfort: $99-$149.
- Trigger Moment: Looks at their JBL Clip or Charge in their bedroom and feels it doesn't belong there. Or visits a friend's place, sees a Marshall on the shelf, feels want-recognition. Then goes to YouTube and falls down a rabbit hole.
- Last Updated: 2026-03-18

## The Occasion Gifter

- Persona Name: The Occasion Gifter
- Product Line: Hako Nomad
- Age Range: 28-50 (buyer); recipient often 20-35
- Key Traits: Buying for someone else — partner, sibling, parent, close friend. Budget anchored at $99-$149 by the occasion (birthdays, Christmas). Needs product to look premium and gift well — "I thought about this" impression. Not a deep researcher. Passes through Reddit briefly to get a verdict. Has heard of Marshall and Bose; has not heard of Edifier or Tribit. Brand recognizability or a strong story replaces recognizability. Gifting confidence (not embarrassed if recipient Googles it) is the core emotional need.
- Trigger Moment: A gift deadline is approaching. They search "good speaker gift under $150" or ask on Reddit. Thread recommends Marshall Emberton, Bose Flex 2, and one or two others. They look at all three. Price and appearance are the deciding factors at the final moment.
- Last Updated: 2026-03-18

## The Weekend Tripper

- Persona Name: The Weekend Tripper
- Product Line: Hako Nomad
- Age Range: 23-34
- Key Traits: Goes camping or hiking a few times a year. Has a van, tent, or does car camping at accessible sites. Currently using JBL or Tribit outdoors because "it's what people use." At home, the same speaker looks wrong on the desk. Wants one speaker that works at a campsite and looks good at home. Discovers speakers in r/Bluetooth_Speakers — not in camping subs. Secondary persona — deprioritize for launch. Outdoor angle may become viable after primary segments are established.
- Trigger Moment: At a campsite and someone nearby has a Marshall. It sounds good. It looks better than their JBL. They look it up that night on their phone.
- Last Updated: 2026-03-18
