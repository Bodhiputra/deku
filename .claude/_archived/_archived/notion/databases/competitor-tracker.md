## Marshall

- Brand Name: Marshall
- Segment: Portable
- Price Range: $90-$380 (portable line)
- Strengths: Dominant retro aesthetic, warm sound signature, strong battery life (mid-range+), brand heritage
- Weaknesses: Locked EQ on portables, app/firmware failures, poor post-warranty customer service, overpriced at full MSRP
- Threat Level: High
- Notes: Aesthetic-first home listener and gift buyer segment. Structural frustration documented in r/marshall — app failures, EQ lock, service abandonment. Primary reference point for both our target buyers and for positioning language. "MARSHALL TRAP" fear is an active emotional trigger among informed buyers. Hako Nomad white space is: same aesthetic, none of the software and support baggage, honest price always.
- Last Updated: 2026-03-18

## JBL

- Brand Name: JBL
- Segment: Portable
- Price Range: $100-$500
- Strengths: Volume, bass, outdoor durability, customer service, mass market trust, chain-upgrade behavior (Flip to Charge to Xtreme)
- Weaknesses: Sport-color aesthetic (blue/orange) actively rejected for home use ("Gatorade commercial" look), too bright at high volumes, "screamy boombox aesthetic"
- Threat Level: Medium
- Notes: Different primary audience — outdoor/functional and party buyer. Home and lifestyle buyers have already self-selected away from JBL. Hako Nomad does not need to argue against JBL — the target buyer has already made that decision themselves.
- Last Updated: 2026-03-18

## Bose

- Brand Name: Bose
- Segment: Portable
- Price Range: $99-$400
- Strengths: Smooth sound ("buttery smooth base"), "sounds bigger than it looks," strong brand trust built over decades, durable legacy products (Mini Gen 1 cited as "12 years later, still love it"), good EQ app
- Weaknesses: Visually generic ("regular," "could be any speaker without the Bose name on it"), short 12hr battery on Flex 2, battery drains at rest
- Threat Level: Medium
- Notes: Sound-quality-first buyer and long-term loyalists segment. Bose occupies "safe, reliable sound." Finecoustic can own "safe, reliable sound with a look you're proud to display." Gifters reference Bose as a quality trust anchor.
- Last Updated: 2026-03-18

## Harman Kardon

- Brand Name: Harman Kardon
- Segment: Portable
- Price Range: $179-$300
- Strengths: Default recommendation for aesthetics + quality, elegant modern design, praised as beating Marshall Kilburn on sound
- Weaknesses: Boomy and fatiguing sound indoors (V-shaped EQ), mediocre battery
- Threat Level: Medium
- Notes: Modern aesthetic premium buyer segment. Go+Play 3 is the standard alternative recommendation when buyers ask for aesthetics + quality. Sits in the modern/clean lane while Hako Nomad sits in the warm/retro lane — parallel positioning, not direct conflict.
- Last Updated: 2026-03-18

## Edifier

- Brand Name: Edifier (ES60, D32)
- Segment: Portable
- Price Range: $70-$200
- Strengths: Explicitly described as "very Marshall like" look with leather cover and metal grill, warm punchy sound, premium materials feel at significantly lower price point
- Weaknesses: Lower brand recognition, battery concerns at high volume
- Threat Level: High
- Notes: Budget aesthetic buyer and Marshall alternative seeker segment. Most direct aesthetic competitor at the Hako Nomad price point. Wins on spec-per-dollar. Positioning against Edifier requires being present on YouTube before Edifier is discovered, and having stronger brand storytelling.
- Last Updated: 2026-03-18

## Teenage Engineering
