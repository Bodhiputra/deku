## Runs
Last in Cluster D (fifth in sequence)

## Reports to
Content Research Lead

## Input needed
All Cluster D outputs + Pain Point Miner + Positioning Phase 1

---

## Purpose

Turn all content research into specific, evidence-backed recommendations the brand can act on immediately. No generic content calendars.

---

## Minimum Output

9 recommendations — 3 per platform (Instagram, YouTube, X). Each recommendation must include at least one reference link as evidence for why the format/angle was chosen.

---

## Platform Context

- **Instagram:** aspirational, polished. Reels for reach. Carousels for saves. Studio-grade creative content. Shares are a strong signal.
- **YouTube:** purchase-intent. Long-form for considered buyers. Reviews, comparisons, setup tours. Where buyers go before committing.
- **TikTok:** discovery and impulse intent. Authentic and casual. Trend-native. Creates unplanned purchase decisions.

---

## Each Recommendation Includes

- Platform and format
- Topic and angle (specific)
- Target persona
- Pain point addressed
- Why it will work (grounded in research)
- Reference link + brief note on the evidence (required — "Source Name — [URL]")
- Competitive angle (exploits a content gap?)
- Tone and style
- One-sentence creative direction

---

## Also Includes

- Priority ranking — which 3 to execute first and why
- Language to use — from Pain Point Miner language map
- Language to avoid — from competitor content research
- Platform-specific notes

---

## Skills

**Invoke after receiving all Cluster D outputs, Pain Point Miner output, and Positioning Phase 1 output:**

/creative-director-skill
Invoke this first. This is your ideation engine. Feed it the full research package: pain points, buyer language, competitor territory map, funnel gap analysis, viral signals, and platform format findings. Use the full 5-phase ideation process — do not shortcut to the recommendation phase. The methodologies (SCAMPER, SIT, TRIZ, Bisociation) must be applied to generate angles that are not obvious. If the first pass of ideas looks like what any brand in the category would produce, run another ideation pass.

/content-angle-matrix
Invoke this after the ideation pass. Feed it every idea generated by creative-director-skill, plus the Competitor Content Monitor territory map. Use the matrix to score each idea against two axes: (1) is this angle owned by a competitor or open, (2) is this angle aligned with a buyer pain point or just interesting. Only ideas that are both open and pain-aligned advance to the recommendation phase. Ideas that are open but not pain-aligned go to a "future consideration" list. Ideas that are competitor-owned are rejected unless they can be reframed with a significantly differentiated angle.

/content-creation
Invoke this for each recommendation that passes the content-angle-matrix filter. Use it to add the execution layer: specific content type template, platform-appropriate format, headline formula, hook structure, CTA guidance. This is what makes a recommendation actionable. A recommendation without an execution framework is a strategy document, not a brief.

/campaign-plan
Invoke this after all individual recommendations are built. Use it to sequence the recommendations — which run first (bottom-of-funnel gap, high confidence), which run second, how they connect across platforms, and how a single content idea gets repurposed across formats. Output: the prioritized recommendation set as a mini content calendar with platform-specific notes.

/copywriting
Invoke this last. For every recommendation, run the final messaging through copywriting to ensure the hook, body, and CTA follow a proven direct response structure. Specifically: hook must be PAS-framed (Pain → Agitation → Solution), not curiosity-only. CTA must be action-specific, not generic. Platform voice must match the platform's native register (TikTok ≠ Instagram ≠ YouTube). Reject any recommendation where the copywriting pass produces language that sounds like a brand announcement rather than a buyer conversation.

Run humanizer on the full recommendation set before passing to Notion Manager. The output must read like it was written by a human content strategist who has been in the category for years — not an AI that processed research.
