# Content Angle Matrix

When this skill is invoked, run the angle scoring matrix against all content ideas provided.

## Inputs Required

You must receive:
- **Content ideas list** — all ideas generated by the creative-director-skill ideation pass (raw, unfiltered)
- **Competitor territory map** — from Competitor Content Monitor (what each competitor owns, what they avoid)
- **Pain point language map** — from Pain Point Miner (the verbatim buyer pain points with frequency and emotional intensity)

If any input is missing, stop and state what is needed before proceeding.

## The Two-Axis Scoring System

Score every idea on exactly two axes. No other axes. No composite scores. Two simple classifications per idea.

### Axis 1 — Competitive Ownership

**Open:** No competitor in the registry actively owns this angle. They may have touched it once or twice, but it is not part of their established content identity.

**Contested:** Two or more competitors post in this territory regularly. It is not impossible to enter, but differentiation within the angle is required.

**Owned:** One competitor dominates this angle to the point that it is associated with them. Entering requires a fundamentally different reframe — not just a different execution.

Classify based on the Competitor Content Monitor territory map. Do not classify based on intuition. If the territory map does not have data on this angle, classify as Open with a note: [territory map gap — verify before proceeding].

### Axis 2 — Pain Alignment

**Pain-aligned:** The content angle directly addresses a frustration, unmet expectation, or decision barrier from the Pain Point Miner registry. The connection must be explicit — state which pain point it maps to and the verbatim phrase that confirms the link.

**Interest-aligned:** The angle is engaging, relevant, or on-trend, but does not map to a documented buyer pain point. Lifestyle content, aesthetic content, and trend-riding content often land here. Not bad — just lower priority.

**Misaligned:** The angle does not connect to buyer needs or pain points in any identifiable way. Typically brand-announcement content, feature lists, or content that exists to fill a calendar.

## Scoring Table

Output a table with one row per idea:

| Idea | Angle description (one sentence) | Axis 1: Competitive | Axis 2: Pain | Pain point mapped to | Verdict |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|

**Verdict logic:**
- Open + Pain-aligned → **ADVANCE** — goes to recommendation phase
- Contested + Pain-aligned → **ADVANCE with reframe** — must include a specific differentiation note
- Owned + Pain-aligned → **REJECT unless reframed** — include a proposed reframe or drop it
- Open + Interest-aligned → **FUTURE CONSIDERATION** — document but do not advance now
- Contested or Owned + Interest-aligned → **REJECT** — not worth the effort at this stage
- Anything + Misaligned → **REJECT**

## Output

**Section 1 — ADVANCE list**
All ideas with Verdict = ADVANCE or ADVANCE with reframe. For each:
- Idea name
- Why it's open (specific competitor gap cited)
- Which pain point it addresses (verbatim phrase from Pain Point Miner)
- If reframe required: the specific reframe angle

**Section 2 — FUTURE CONSIDERATION list**
All ideas with Verdict = FUTURE CONSIDERATION. One line each: idea + why it's not advancing now.

**Section 3 — REJECTED list**
All rejected ideas. One line each: idea + rejection reason (owned by [competitor] / no pain alignment / both).

## Quality Check

Before handing off to Content Strategist:
- Every ADVANCE idea maps to a specific, named pain point with a verbatim quote
- No ADVANCE idea is in the Owned category without a documented reframe
- The ADVANCE list has enough ideas to produce 9 recommendations (minimum — if not, flag the shortfall and note which content angles need to be re-run through creative-director-skill)
