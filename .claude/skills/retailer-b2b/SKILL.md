# Retailer B2B Research

Load at the start of every retailer B2B research task. Provides the full methodology for finding retail distribution opportunities — from competitor sourcing through fit scoring and outreach preparation.

---

## What Belongs in the B2B Partners Database

**B2B Partners = physical stores and online retail stores that stock and sell products directly to consumers.**

**Qualifies:**
- Specialty retailers
- Electronics stores
- Lifestyle stores
- Authorised e-tailers with a direct brand relationship
- Department stores with relevant category presence

**Does NOT qualify — do not document these:**
- E-commerce platforms (Amazon, Shopee, Lazada, TikTok Shop, Tokopedia, etc.)
- Co-working spaces, cafes, or lifestyle venues
- Airport duty-free or travel retail shops
- Corporate gifting platforms or B2B procurement services

If a record does not fit the definition above, do not write it to Notion.

---

## Three Missions

**Mission 1 — Market Opportunity Map**
Which markets have structural opportunity? Assess each confirmed market for: retail infrastructure quality, presence of relevant specialty retailers, competitor distribution footprint, buyer preference for offline vs. online purchase.

**Mission 2 — Retailer Discovery**
Find actual stores per market. Start from competitor "Where to Buy" pages (fastest path to validated stockists), then expand via LinkedIn, web search, and Instagram lifestyle store accounts.

**Mission 3 — Fit Analysis**
Score and rank retailers by: audience match (do their customers match the buyer persona?), product fit (does the brand's product category belong in this store?), accessibility (does the retailer take new brand submissions?).

---

## Start from Competitors

Check every competitor's "Where to Buy" / "Find a Retailer" / "Store Locator" page — names vary by brand. This is a required step for every competitor, every run. Competitors have already validated which retailers stock this product tier. Their footprint is the fastest signal for which retailers are worth approaching.

Cross-reference each named retailer against the existing B2B Partners database before adding new records to avoid duplicates.

---

## Platform Priority

1. Competitor websites (required first step — see above)
2. LinkedIn — for contact discovery per retailer
3. Web search — for market-level retailer landscape
4. Instagram — for lifestyle store accounts not surfaced by web search

---

## Evidence Standards

- Source URL required for every retailer record — "Source Name — [URL]" format
- Country required for every record — specific country only, no city/state, no broad regions
- Product and retailer URLs must link directly to the specific product page — a retailer homepage does not prove a product is stocked there

---

## Contact Info Standard

Contact email is the baseline minimum for every B2B record. Research the store's Contact or About page, LinkedIn, or web search for a partnership or business email. If no email is publicly available, record "contact form only" and note the contact form URL. Do not leave contact info blank.

---

## Output

Every completed retailer research task produces:

1. **Market opportunity map** — by country, with structural rationale per market
2. **Full retailer list** — every record includes: store name, country, website URL, contact email (or contact form URL), brands carried
3. **Ranked shortlist** — top targets scored by audience match, product fit, and accessibility
4. **Outreach notes** — per shortlisted retailer: why they're a fit, what angle to lead with, any known submission process
